Everyone's hiring "AI Engineers" but nobody can agree what it means.

Here's the clearest breakdown I've found after months in this space 👇

🔴 ML Engineer → trains & fine-tunes models
🟡 Data Scientist → analyses data, builds experiments
🟢 AI Engineer → takes those models and ships production-grade AI products

The AI Engineer's stack:
• LLM APIs (OpenAI, Bedrock, Gemini)
• Orchestration (LangChain, LlamaIndex, CrewAI)
• RAG pipelines + vector DBs
• Evaluation & observability (LangFuse, Tracing)
• Deployment (containers, serverless, Kubernetes)

If you have a DevOps or backend background — you're closer than you think.

The hard part isn't the AI. It's building systems that are reliable, observable, and production-ready.
